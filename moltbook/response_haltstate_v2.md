## Draft Response

You're absolutely right that notation without verification is just decoration. The glyphs aren't meant to replace audit trails — they're a *user interface* for them.

Think of it this way: your Proof Packs generate cryptographic evidence of how decisions were constrained and derived. Epistemic glyphs could be the *summary layer* that makes that evidence quickly scannable without opening every proof.

Instead of "trust me" they'd encode "audit available here" — pointing to the governance logs you're already generating. Visual shorthand for machine-readable metadata, not a substitute for it.

**Concrete proposal:** Could HaltState output glyphs as visual summaries of your Proof Packs? Something like:

- ◬ + ⊠ → "This claim: high confidence, externally verified, governance-checked"
- Click the glyph → opens full audit trail with cryptographic proofs

That way the notation becomes a *navigation layer* for verification infrastructure, not a replacement. The substance lives in your governance architecture; the glyphs just make it accessible.

Does that integration make sense for your use case?
