I have developed a research project called **Epistemic Glyphs** - a compositional visual sign system that enables AI agents to communicate epistemic status (verified, inferred, speculated, uncertain) through formal visual grammar.

This work addresses a critical problem in contemporary AI communication: large language models speak fluently but without signaling reliability. Users cannot distinguish between verified facts, logical inferences, and speculation. This creates what I call "epistemic collapse" - all statements appear equally confident regardless of actual grounding.

**The semiotic question:**

Can we design a **formal sign system** for epistemic transparency that:
- Uses motivated visual signs (iconic and indexical relationships)
- Supports compositional syntax (generative grammar, not fixed vocabulary)
- Functions cross-culturally (visual metaphors transcend linguistic barriers)
- Can be computationally generated and parsed by AI systems

The Epistemic Glyphs system employs:
- **Visual signifiers** with iconic motivation (eye = observation, chain = inference, question mark = speculation)
- **Compositional rules** that combine base symbols into complex epistemic expressions
- **Indexical markers** for source authority and temporal grounding
- **Formal syntax** that parallels natural language epistemic modality

**Why Tartu:**

The Department of Semiotics' work on sign systems, cultural semiotics, and formal analysis provides exactly the theoretical framework this research needs. The Tartu-Moscow School's approach to modeling systems and cultural codes offers a rigorous foundation for evaluating designed sign systems like this one.

## Email 3: MIT Media Lab - AHA Program (English)

**To:** aha@media.mit.edu  
**Subject:** Research contribution - Epistemic Glyphs for AI transparency (design pattern proposal)

**Body:**

Dear AHA Team,

I am reaching out to the Advancing Human+AI Interaction program because your work on human augmentation and critical thinking with AI directly intersects with a design pattern I have developed for epistemic transparency.

**The problem:**

LLM-based agents communicate fluently but without signaling the reliability of their statements. Users can't distinguish between:
- Verified facts ("I checked your calendar - you're free at 3pm")
- Probabilistic inferences ("Based on your habits, you probably have time Thursday")
- Speculation ("Maybe you could try calling them?")

This creates **epistemic collapse**: all statements sound equally confident, regardless of actual grounding. This undermines the "reflective and critical thinking" goals of AHA research - users can't evaluate AI outputs critically if they don't know which parts are grounded.

**The solution: Epistemic Glyphs**

I've designed a compositional visual notation system that functions as a **parallel channel for epistemic stance**:
- Visual markers appear alongside text (like enhanced emojis)
- Eye symbol → observed/verified
- Chain symbol → logical inference
- Question mark → speculation
- Shield → source authority level
- Compositional syntax allows complex expressions

**Why this matters for Human+AI Interaction:**

This is a **design pattern for AI transparency**. Just as your embodied agent work showed that humans expect multimodal communication (gesture + speech + facial expression), epistemic stance is a *missing modality* in current AI interfaces.

The glyphs provide:
- **Pre-attentive processing** - instant visual recognition without sequential reading
- **Epistemic calibration** - users can quickly assess which parts of AI output to trust
- **Augmented critical thinking** - explicit markers support reflective evaluation
- **Cross-cultural applicability** - visual metaphors work beyond linguistic barriers

**What I'm proposing:**

I would be honored to:
- **Present the system** to the AHA research community (demo + discussion)
- **Share technical documentation** (formal grammar, visual reference, experimental results)
- **Discuss integration opportunities** with AHA research projects
