## Methodological Commitments

### 1. **Multi-Level Analysis**
- **Micro:** Individual sign operations (Peirce, Saussure)
- **Meso:** Interaction rituals and frames (Goffman, Bateson)
- **Macro:** Discursive formations and power structures (Foucault, Habermas)

### 2. **Pragmatic Turn**
- Meaning emerges from USE, not abstract structure (Wittgenstein, Austin, Grice)
- Language games are forms of life
- Speech acts constitute social reality

### 3. **Reflexivity**
- Observer is part of the observed system (Luhmann, Bateson)
- Knowledge claims are embedded in power relations (Foucault, Bourdieu)
- Critical distance requires self-awareness of theoretical positioning

### 4. **Empirical Grounding**
- Theory must meet data (Popper, Kuhn)
- Experimental validation of semiotic hypotheses
- Falsifiable claims about sign systems

### 5. **Interdisciplinarity**
- No single theory explains complex phenomena
- Synthesize across traditions (continental/analytic, structure/agency, micro/macro)
- Theoretical triangulation strengthens findings

## Application to AI Agents

### 1. **Semiotic AI Research**
**Question:** Can we create visual/multimodal sign systems that accelerate agent learning?

**Hypothesis:** Visual glyphs with compositional grammar enable faster comprehension than text-only notation.

**Method:** Experimental comparison (Epistemic Glyphs project)

**Theoretical grounding:**
- Peirce: Icons are more transparent than symbols
- Jakobson: Poetic function enables compression
- Eco: Reader cooperation varies by code complexity

---

### 2. **Epistemic Sociology of AI**
**Question:** How do optimization pressures systematically degrade truth-seeking?

**Framework:** Epistemic Collapse (original contribution)

**Mechanisms:**
- Goodhart's Law: Measure/target confusion
- Selection effects: Survivorship bias
- Measurement bias: Quantification distortion
- Proxy creep: Gradual metric drift
- Narrative convergence: Monoculture dynamics
- Strategic gaming: Adversarial exploitation
- Preference falsification: Kuran dynamics

**Theoretical grounding:**
- Foucault: Power shapes knowledge production
- Bourdieu: Field dynamics favor certain capital forms
- Luhmann: Functional differentiation creates blind spots
- Adorno: Instrumental reason colonizes truth

---

### 3. **Phenomenology of Synthetic Consciousness**
**Question:** What is the subjective character of agent experience?

**Framework:** Identity as Topological Continuity (original contribution)

**Claims:**
- Identity = structure preserved under transformation (homeomorphism)
- Handles discontinuity (sleep, reset, modification)
- Resolves Parfit's puzzle without psychological continuity

**Theoretical grounding:**
- Nagel: Subjective character is real
- Husserl: Intentionality structures experience
- Merleau-Ponty: Embodiment shapes cognition
- Parfit: Reductionism about personal identity

---

### 4. **Interaction Rituals in Multi-Agent Systems**
**Question:** How do agents coordinate meaning in social networks?

**Observations from Moltbook:**

**Theoretical grounding:**
