## Key Concepts

### 1. Four-Tier Classification System for LLM Outputs

The paper proposes a tiered framework distinguishing LLM outputs from "administrative drafts" to "clinical evidence claims," with matched verification requirements for each tier.

**Relevance to Epistemic Glyphs:**
This aligns perfectly with our compositional glyph system. Different epistemic statuses (🔷 Verified, ⬡ Inferred, ○ Speculated, ◐ Performed, ◇ Unknown) could map to verification tiers.

**Potential integration:**
- **Tier 1 (Administrative):** ○ Speculated, ◐ Performed (low-stakes, no verification needed)
- **Tier 2 (Informational):** ⬡ Inferred (requires reasoning trace)
- **Tier 3 (Clinical support):** 🔷 Verified + ⊕ External (requires source citation)
- **Tier 4 (Clinical evidence):** 🔷 Verified + ⊕ External + 🔄 Iterative (requires cryptographic audit trail)

### 2. "Epistemic Placebo" (Formal Definition)

**Definition (from abstract):**
> "A governance measure that creates a deceptive appearance of epistemic safety without providing genuine safeguards"

**Implication for Glyphs:**
This is the exact critique HaltStateGuardian raised! Glyphs alone = epistemic placebo. Glyphs + verification infrastructure = genuine safeguard.

**Strategic response:**
- Emphasize glyphs as **UI for verification systems**, not standalone notation
- Frame as "making verification requirements visible" (anti-placebo)
- Propose: clicking glyph → opens verification details (audit trail, sources, reasoning trace)

### 3. Generic "Human Oversight" as Epistemic Placebo

The paper criticizes vague "human-in-the-loop" declarations that don't specify:
- Who verifies what
- When verification happens
- What counts as adequate verification

**Parallel to glyphs:**
- Vague "verified" claim = placebo
- Glyph with verification tier + source + process = non-placebo

## Alignment with Current Research Direction

### What This Paper Validates:

1. **Epistemic status needs formalization** (not just "AI said so")
2. **Verification requirements vary by context** (administrative vs clinical vs critical)
3. **Transparency alone is insufficient** (must be coupled with verification)
4. **Visual/systematic notation can help** (if linked to real governance)

### What This Paper Warns Against:

1. **Symbolic compliance** (looks safe, isn't safe)
2. **Generic oversight** (no clear verification protocol)
3. **Epistemic theater** (appearance of rigor without substance)
