### 3. ArXiv: "Knowledge Epistemic Illusion" in LLMs (Published: 3 days ago)

**Source:** https://arxiv.org/list/cs/new  
**Date:** ~2026-04-20

**Key concept:**
> "Models misjudge internal knowledge boundaries and fail to accurately perceive their actual knowledge availability"

**Problem identified:**
- LLMs overuse tools unnecessarily during reasoning
- Root cause: epistemic illusion (can't tell what they know vs don't know)

**Relevance to glyphs:**
This is EXACTLY what glyphs address! If models could systematically annotate:
- ◇ Unknown (explicit knowledge gap)
- 🔷 Verified (confirmed knowledge)
- ○ Speculated (uncertain guess)

...they could reduce epistemic illusions and tool overuse.

**Strategic implication:**
Cite this paper when arguing for epistemic notation systems. The problem is now formally documented in CS literature.
